Synadentix Ingredients: What Is Actually in the Formula?

The synadentix ingredients list is one of the primary reasons this product stands out in the crowded dental supplement market. Rather than relying on generic multivitamin blends, the formula is built around clinically researched compounds that target distinct pathways in oral health. Here is a breakdown of the key active components.

Microcrystalline Hydroxyapatite
Enamel Remineralization

This advanced mineral compound closely mirrors the natural structure of human tooth enamel. Research suggests it can fill microscopic surface defects and reduce sensitivity by restoring the mineral density of weakened enamel. It is considered one of the most biomimetic remineralizing agents currently available in supplement form.

Lactoferrin
Selective Antimicrobial

A naturally occurring glycoprotein found in healthy saliva and breast milk, lactoferrin binds to iron, starving pathogenic bacteria of a mineral they need to thrive. Unlike broad-spectrum antiseptics, clinical evidence indicates that lactoferrin is selective, targeting harmful organisms while leaving beneficial oral bacteria intact.

Dextranase and Beta-Glucanase
Biofilm Disruption

These two enzymes are specifically designed to break down the sugar-based scaffolding (glucan polymers) that allows plaque to adhere to tooth surfaces and gumlines. Studies show that enzymatic disruption of biofilm is significantly more effective than mechanical brushing alone, particularly in interproximal areas.

Vitamin C (as Ascorbic Acid)
Gum Collagen Support

Vitamin C is essential for the synthesis of collagen, the structural protein that maintains the integrity and firmness of gum tissue. Research consistently shows that low dietary vitamin C is correlated with increased gum vulnerability. Supplementation helps support tissue resilience and repair after minor gum irritation.

Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10)
Gum Antioxidant Defense

CoQ10 is a powerful cellular antioxidant that has been studied specifically for its role in periodontal health. Clinical studies have found notably low levels of CoQ10 in the gum tissue of patients with gum disease. Supplementation appears to reduce oxidative stress in periodontal tissue and may help soothe chronic gum inflammation.

Zinc (as Zinc Citrate)
Microbial Balance

Zinc has well-documented anti-plaque and anti-inflammatory properties in the oral cavity. It works in part by inhibiting the growth of volatile sulfur compound-producing bacteria, which are the primary cause of halitosis. Research also suggests zinc supports white blood cell activity in gum tissue, contributing to local immune defense.

Xylitol
Anti-Cariogenic Agent

Xylitol, a naturally derived sugar alcohol, has a well-established evidence base in dental research. Streptococcus mutans, the primary bacteria responsible for tooth decay, cannot metabolize xylitol and gradually dies off in its presence. Regular xylitol exposure has been shown to reduce cavity-causing bacteria counts significantly over time.

Guava Fruit Powder
Anti-Inflammatory Botanical

Guava leaf and fruit extracts have been used in traditional oral medicine for generations and are now supported by modern research. They contain quercetin, kaempferol, and other polyphenols that exert anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects on gum tissue. Studies suggest guava extract helps reduce gum bleeding and supports mucosal comfort.

Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol)
Calcium Absorption Activator

Without adequate Vitamin D3, the body cannot efficiently absorb and utilize calcium for bone and tooth mineralization. D3 also plays a direct immune-modulating role in gum tissue, and research links D3 deficiency to increased periodontal inflammation. Its inclusion in the Synadentix formula ensures the mineral components are bioavailable.

Lysozyme
Natural Antibacterial Enzyme

Lysozyme is a natural enzyme present in healthy saliva that attacks the cell walls of gram-positive bacteria. It is an important component of the mouth's innate immune defense system. Its inclusion in the synadentix formula helps amplify the natural antibacterial capacity of saliva, particularly during the reduced-flow overnight period.
